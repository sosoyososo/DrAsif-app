Part 1 of 5

Missing Backend Services

The app has no server. Everything runs on the user's phone with no data leaving the device — which means several features cannot actually work.

🔐
User Accounts & Login
Users cannot create an account or sign in
Critical

The app has no concept of who a user is. There is no sign-up, no sign-in, no password, no email verification.

🚫 What happens: If a user gets a new phone, deletes the app, or clears their data — all progress, weight history, food logs, journals, and challenge data are gone forever. No recovery. No backup. No way to get it back.
☁️
Cloud Data Backup & Sync
All data is device-only with zero redundancy
Critical

Weight logs, meal entries, daily check-ins, journal entries, challenge progress, body measurements — every single piece of user data exists only in the phone's local storage. There is nowhere else it is saved.

📱 What happens: A broken phone, a lost device, or even just reinstalling the app means months of data vanish instantly. This generates very angry reviews and support requests.
🤖
AI Coach — Missing API Key
The AI chat feature does not work
Critical

The AI Coach (chat assistant) and the food photo scanner both call Anthropic's AI service — but the code has no API key configured. Every request will fail with an authentication error.

💥 What happens: Two of the app's headline features are completely broken. Users who tap "Ask the AI Coach" or "Scan My Food" will always see an error. This will result in very poor App Store ratings.
🛡️
AI API Key Security Proxy
Embedding the API key in the app is dangerous
Critical

Even once you add the API key, putting it directly in the app code is a major security risk. Anyone with basic technical skills can extract it from the installed application.

💰 What happens: Someone extracts your AI key and uses it for their own purposes. You pay the bill — potentially thousands of dollars in a single night, with no way to stop it. A backend proxy server is the only safe solution.
👥
Real Community Content Moderation
The "admin approval" system is fake
Critical

The Community tab lets users submit weight-loss stories. The app says "your story will be reviewed in 24–48 hours" — but there is no one to review it. No backend, no admin panel. Submissions simply sit on the user's phone, never seen by anyone.

😤 What happens: Users submit stories and nothing ever happens. They feel misled. Meanwhile, Apple and Google both require that apps with user-submitted content have real moderation tools (approve, reject, delete, report, block). Without these, the app will be rejected.
💳
RevenueCat — Subscription & Payment System
No way to charge users for the app
Critical

The app currently has zero payment code. If the plan is to charge users (either a subscription or a one-time purchase), RevenueCat needs to be integrated to handle billing through Apple and Google's systems.

🚫 What happens: The app is free for everyone with no way to collect payment. Apple and Google require all digital purchases to go through their billing — there is no alternative payment method allowed.
🔔
Push Notifications
No way to re-engage users
High

The app cannot send any notifications — no reminders to log meals, no encouragement to keep going, no "your story was approved" alerts.

📉 What happens: Users download the app, use it for a day, and forget about it. Without gentle reminders, daily active users drop fast and retention is low.
📊
Usage Analytics
No visibility into how users behave
High

There is no analytics system. You have no way to know how many people are using the app, which features they use, or where they get stuck and quit.

🤷 What happens: You make product decisions completely blind. Is the AI Coach useful? Do people complete the 3-week challenge? Where do they give up? Without data, you have to guess — and guesses are often wrong.
🖼️
Food Image Upload — No Server Endpoint
Images are sent directly to Anthropic, no fallback
High

The food scanner sends photos directly to Anthropic's API from the app. There is no server in between to resize images, log usage, handle errors gracefully, or cache results.

🔄 What happens: Every scan call goes directly to Anthropic. If the AI service is temporarily down, the user gets a broken experience. There is no retry logic, no image compression (large photos = more cost), and no usage tracking.

App Store & Google Play Compliance

Issues that Apple and Google reviewers will flag when you submit. Some will cause an outright rejection; others must be fixed to pass review.

📄
No Privacy Policy URL
Required by both Apple and Google to submit
Critical

The app has a short privacy notice inside it, but both stores require a link to a full privacy policy published on a website. This is a hard requirement for submission.

🚫 What happens: You literally cannot submit the app to either store without providing a privacy policy URL. The submission form will not let you proceed.
📷
Missing iOS Camera & Photo Permissions
Info.plist does not declare required permissions
Critical

The food scanner uses the camera and photo library, but the iOS configuration file is missing NSCameraUsageDescription and NSPhotoLibraryUsageDescription. On an iPhone, tapping "Take Photo" will crash the app immediately.

💥 What happens: The app crashes when a user tries to take a food photo. Apple's automated testing will detect this crash and reject the submission outright.
⚖️
Weight-Loss Testimonials with Specific Numbers
"Lost 16 kg in 3 months" — high regulatory risk
Critical

The Community tab displays testimonials with specific weight-loss outcomes. Apple and Google heavily scrutinize health apps that show before/after-type claims. Even though these are real book reviews, displayed in an app they create regulatory risk.

⚠️ What happens: Apple may classify the app as making health claims requiring additional approval, or reject it outright. Both stores have strict rules about health outcome claims in apps.
🩺
AI Coach — May Be Seen as Medical Advice
Discusses GLP-1 drugs, symptoms, BMI targets
High

The AI Coach answers questions about prescription medications (Ozempic, Wegovy), describes clinical trial data, lists pancreatitis symptoms, and gives BMI classifications. Apple may view this as the app providing medical advice.

🔍 What happens: Apple may reject the app or ask for evidence of medical review. At minimum, stronger disclaimers about AI limitations are needed, and the AI's responses should be reviewed by a medical professional.
⛑️
Medical Cross Symbol in Logo
Legally protected symbol in most countries
High

The app logo uses a red medical cross symbol. The red cross is a protected symbol under the Geneva Conventions and its use in commercial products is restricted in the UK, US, EU, and most other countries.

⚖️ What happens: Apple may flag this during review. More importantly, using a protected medical symbol in a commercial wellness app carries legal risk. Recommendation: replace with a different health-related icon (e.g. a leaf, heart, or abstract mark).
👮
No User Reporting or Content Blocking
Cannot moderate or report community content
High

If someone submits inappropriate content to the community, there is no way for users to report it and no way for you to remove it. Apple requires apps with community features to include reporting and blocking.

🚫 What happens: Apple will reject the app for having user-generated content without moderation and reporting tools.
📜
No Terms of Service / EULA
No legal agreement between you and your users
Medium

The app has a medical disclaimer but no formal End User License Agreement or Terms of Service.

⚖️ What happens: Less legal protection if a user claims they were misled, if there's a dispute about subscription refunds, or if content in the app causes any issues.
🗑️
No Data Export or Account Deletion
GDPR and CCPA requirement
Medium

Users cannot export their data or request account deletion. If you add cloud backup (Phase 1), EU and California privacy laws legally require you to provide these options.

⚖️ What happens: Risk of fines and legal action under GDPR (Europe) and CCPA (California) if you launch without data export and deletion capabilities.
🧒
No Age Rating or Children's Privacy
COPPA / children's online privacy
Medium

The app collects health data and has community features but has no age gate or children's privacy protections. Apple and Google require age ratings and have specific rules for apps that could be used by children.

👶 What happens: The app needs an age rating (likely 12+ or 17+ due to medical content). If children under 13 could potentially use it, additional COPPA compliance measures are needed.
📋
Apple Privacy Questionnaire Not Completed
Must disclose data collection practices
Medium

Apple now requires all apps to complete a privacy questionnaire in App Store Connect, detailing what data is collected and how it is used. This must be filled out before submission.

📝 What happens: Without completing this questionnaire, the app cannot be submitted. This is a newer Apple requirement that catches many first-time developers off guard.
📱
Google Play Data Safety Section
Must disclose data handling practices
Medium

Google Play requires a "Data Safety" section in the store listing that explains what data the app collects, whether it's shared with third parties (e.g. Anthropic for AI features), and whether it's encrypted.

📝 What happens: The app cannot be published on Google Play without completing the Data Safety form in Google Play Console.

Developer Accounts & Infrastructure

Before you can submit anything to the stores, you need to register as a developer and set up the publishing infrastructure.

🍎
Apple Developer Program Membership
$99/year (or £79/year in the UK)
Critical

You must enrol in the Apple Developer Program to distribute apps on the App Store. It costs $99 per year and requires an Apple ID. Enrolment can take a few days to a few weeks if they need to verify your identity or business details.

  • ☐ Register at developer.apple.com/program/
  • ☐ Choose individual or organisation enrolment
  • ☐ For organisation: have your D-U-N-S number ready (business verification)
🚫 What happens: Without an Apple Developer account, you cannot submit to the App Store at all. No workaround.
▶️
Google Play Developer Account
$25 one-time fee
Critical

A Google Play Developer account costs a one-time fee of $25. Registration is usually faster than Apple's.

  • ☐ Register at play.google.com/console/
  • ☐ Pay the $25 registration fee
🚫 What happens: Without a Google Play Developer account, you cannot publish on Google Play.
🔑
App Signing Certificates & Provisioning Profiles
Required to build a distributable iOS app
Critical

Apple requires all iOS apps to be digitally signed with a certificate before they can be installed on devices or submitted to the App Store. This involves creating certificates, app IDs, and provisioning profiles in the Apple Developer portal.

  • ☐ Create a Certificate Signing Request (CSR)
  • ☐ Generate distribution certificate in Apple Developer portal
  • ☐ Create App ID with the correct bundle identifier
  • ☐ Generate provisioning profile for App Store distribution
  • ☐ Configure code signing in Xcode project
🚫 What happens: Without valid signing, Xcode will refuse to build the app for distribution, and App Store Connect will reject unsigned uploads.
☕
Android App Signing Key
Required to publish on Google Play
Critical

Google Play requires Android apps to be signed with a keystore. You can use Google's Play App Signing (recommended) or manage your own key.

  • ☐ Generate a keystore for app signing (or use Google Play App Signing)
  • ☐ Configure build.gradle with signing config
🧪
TestFlight & Internal Testing Setup
Apple's beta testing platform
High

Before submitting to Apple review, you should test the app via TestFlight (Apple's beta testing platform). This allows you to distribute the app to internal and external testers before going live.

  • ☐ Set up TestFlight in App Store Connect
  • ☐ Add internal testers (up to 100 team members via email)
  • ☐ Optional: Set up external testing for beta users (requires review but less strict)
⚙️
Push Notification Certificates (Apple)
Required for push notifications to iPhone users
High

If push notifications are implemented, Apple requires an APNs (Apple Push Notification service) certificate or key to be configured in the Developer Portal.

  • ☐ Create APNs key in Apple Developer portal
  • ☐ Configure the push notification capability in Xcode
  • ☐ Set up Firebase Cloud Messaging or similar for push delivery (if using cross-platform)
📝
Export Compliance Documentation (Apple)
Encryption declaration for App Store submission
High

Apple requires all apps to declare whether they use encryption. Since the app connects to servers (once the backend is built) and the AI features use HTTPS, you need to answer the export compliance questionnaire.

  • ☐ Complete the Export Compliance section in App Store Connect
  • ☐ If using standard HTTPS only, you can self-classify under Category 5 Part 2

Priority Action Plan

The recommended order of work. Each phase builds on the previous one.

Immediate

Phase 0 — Quick Wins (1–3 days)

Fix legal and compliance blockers that take minimal time.
  • 1 Register domain and set up a simple website
  • 2 Write and publish the Privacy Policy page on the website
  • 3 Write Terms of Service / EULA
  • 4 Add camera and photo library permission descriptions to iOS Info.plist
  • 5 Redesign the app icon to remove the medical cross symbol
  • 6 Soften or add disclaimers around weight-loss testimonials
  • 7 Add stronger AI limitations disclaimer to the Coach feature
  • 8 Register Apple Developer Program ($99/year)
  • 9 Register Google Play Developer account ($25 one-time)
Weeks 1–4

Phase 1 — Backend Server (3–5 weeks)

Build the server that makes the app actually work.
  • 1 User authentication system (email/password or social login)
  • 2 Cloud database for user data backup and sync
  • 3 Secure AI API proxy server (Anthropic key stays on server, not in app)
  • 4 Admin panel for community story moderation (approve/reject/delete)
  • 5 User reporting system for community content
  • 6 Push notification infrastructure
  • 7 Usage analytics system
Weeks 4–7

Phase 2 — Payments & Store Assets (2–3 weeks)

Add monetisation and prepare all store submission materials.
  • 1 Integrate RevenueCat SDK for App Store and Google Play billing
  • 2 Design and implement paywall screens
  • 3 Add data export and account deletion features (GDPR/CCPA)
  • 4 Design and generate all app icon sizes
  • 5 Design and generate App Store screenshots (all display sizes)
  • 6 Write App Store description, subtitle, keywords, and promotional text
  • 7 Write Google Play short description and full description
  • 8 Design Google Play feature graphic (1024×500 px)
  • 9 Complete Apple privacy questionnaire in App Store Connect
  • 10 Complete Google Play Data Safety section
  • 11 Complete Apple age rating and Google Play content rating questionnaires
  • 12 Prepare demo account for reviewers
  • 13 Fill in all store metadata (copyright, support URL, category, etc.)
Weeks 7–8

Phase 3 — Certificates & Signing (1 week)

Set up code signing infrastructure for distribution.
  • 1 Create Apple distribution certificate and provisioning profiles
  • 2 Create Android keystore or configure Play App Signing
  • 3 Configure code signing in Xcode and Android build
  • 4 Complete Apple export compliance questionnaire
  • 5 Set up TestFlight with internal testers
  • 6 Test the full app flow via TestFlight and internal APK
Weeks 8–10

Phase 4 — Launch & Iterate (1–2 weeks)

Submit, respond to feedback, and go live.
  • 1 End-to-end testing with the new backend on real devices
  • 2 Submit to Apple App Store via App Store Connect
  • 3 Submit to Google Play via Google Play Console
  • 4 Monitor for reviewer feedback, fix any issues, resubmit
  • 5 Once approved — celebrate the launch! 🎉

⏱️ Total Estimated Timeline

8–12 weeks for a small team (1–2 backend developers + 1 frontend/mobile developer).
Phase 0 can be done immediately. Phase 1 is the heaviest lift. Phases 2 and 3 can run partially in parallel with Phase 1 if the team is well-coordinated.
